Introduction

Monitoring forest fire risks and their impact on wildlife is crucial for the preservation of ecosystems and biodiversity. Forests are essential habitats for a diverse range of species, but they face constant threats like wildfires and deforestation. Utilizing tools such as satellite imagery and fire detection systems enables us to detect changes in forest conditions and take proactive measures. These fires not only destroy habitats but also release carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, contributing to climate change and potentially altering wildlife populations.

Diligent monitoring of risks is essential for safeguarding forests and the well-being of the wildlife that depends on them. This practice plays a crucial role in conserving our natural heritage for future generations by enabling us to comprehend, alleviate, and address the multifaceted challenges presented by forest fires and their impact on wildlife. Through proactive monitoring, we can effectively mitigate these risks and ensure the preservation of forests and their inhabitants for years to come.

Forest Fire Studies of FSI

The Forest Survey of India (FSI) has been at the forefront of forest fire monitoring in the country since the initiation of satellite data-based alerts for near-real-time detection of active forest fires in 2004. The organization provides a range of services aimed at preventing and mitigating the impact of forest fires. These services are instrumental in enhancing early detection, response, and management of forest fires, thereby contributing to the preservation of the country's valuable forest resources.

Services Provided BY FSI:

  • Near Real-Time Forest Fire Monitoring (MODIS & SNPP-VIIRS).
  • Large Forest Fire Monitoring
  • Fire Weather Index base Forest Fire Danger Rating System (FFDRS).
  • Pre Fire Alert (one week advance based on Forest Fire Danger Rating).
  • Van Agni Geo-portal.
  • Fire Prone Forest Area.
  • API and WMS service to State Forest Department.
  • Burnt Scar Assessment.

1. Near Real-Time Forest Fire Monitoring (MODIS & SNPP-VIIRS).

FSI employs advanced satellite technology, including MODIS (Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer) and SNPP-VIIRS (Suomi National Polar-orbiting Partnership Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite), to monitor and detect active forest fires in near-real-time. This capability enables swift response measures to be taken to contain and control the spread of fires.

Learn More.

2. Large Forest Fire Monitoring

FSI extends its monitoring efforts to track large-scale forest fires. This involves the systematic observation and analysis of significant fire incidents, allowing for a comprehensive understanding of the extent and impact of such events on forest ecosystems.

Learn More.

3. Fire Weather Index-based Forest Fire Danger Rating System (FFDRS).

FSI has implemented a Forest Fire Danger Rating System (FFDRS) based on Fire Weather Index, which assesses various meteorological and environmental factors to predict the likelihood of forest fires. This system aids in proactive planning and resource allocation for fire prevention and control.

Learn More.

4. Pre-Fire Alert (One Week Advance based on Forest Fire Danger Rating):

FSI provides pre-fire alerts to relevant authorities with a one-week advance notice based on the Forest Fire Danger Rating. This advance warning allows for preparedness and strategic deployment of resources to areas at higher risk of fire, minimizing the potential damage.

Learn More.

5. Van Agni Geo-portal:

The Van Agni Geo-portal serves as a centralized platform for accessing and visualizing forest fire-related data. This portal facilitates data dissemination, analysis, and decision-making for stakeholders involved in forest fire management.

Learn More.

6. Fire-Prone Forest Area:

FSI identifies and maps fire-prone forest areas, providing valuable information for land-use planning, forest management strategies, and targeted intervention measures to reduce the incidence of fires in vulnerable regions.

Learn More.

7. API and WMS Service to State Forest Department:

FSI offers Application Programming Interface (API) and Web Map Service (WMS) to State Forest Departments, enabling seamless integration of FSI's forest fire monitoring data into the state-level forest management systems. This collaboration enhances the effectiveness of regional efforts in preventing and combating forest fires.

Learn More.

8. Burnt Scar Assessment:

FSI conducts burnt scar assessments to evaluate the aftermath of forest fires. This information is crucial for understanding the ecological impact, planning rehabilitation efforts, and implementing measures to restore affected areas.

Learn More.

In summary ...

the Forest Survey of India's comprehensive approach to forest fire monitoring encompasses real-time detection, danger assessment, pre-fire alerts, mapping of fire-prone areas, and collaborative initiatives with state departments. These efforts collectively contribute to proactive fire management and the sustainable conservation of India's diverse forest ecosystems.
